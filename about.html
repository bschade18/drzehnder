My Medical Journey

For me, there was never one defining moment where I knew I was going to become a practicing physician but it has been nudges in the direction of the vocation I desire. From an early age my cousin-in-law Dr. Berend was one of my role models and I was probably biased since he did cool things like sit on the bench at Duke basketball games. However, for me I was not thinking at the time much about a career but more about being able to shoot hoops in my driveway until my mom turned the lights out on me or playing backyard football with my neighborhood friends. Sports were life, life was sports.

It continued this way into high school where at times I would excel in school in a subject but my eyes were set on achieving an athletic scholarship in football. I loved the game and while I knew I was not going to play professionally I hoped that it would lead me into college into some kind of career field. I shadowed parents, friend’s parents and took career assessments which listed jobs like carpet upholstery and things that I feel like were not in my skillset. I also tested the waters and sent out applications at schools where I thought had cool names and locations: NYU, Pepperdine, etc. After attending visits to Butler and Princeton for football and glancing at Lehigh University in my heart I knew that I was not going to have a future in college football. I began thinking about how I could channel my time and efforts from sports into developing a career. It was when I chose to pursue Miami U for the Chinese business program where I started my collegiate experience. When that first semester did not go as planned, I decided to return home to Ohio State. I took a few classes and was able to work my way into the honors Business program, but there is one thing that distinctly sticks out. I had an advising meeting and it involved what I needed to do to get into the honors business program and laid out four years worth of classes. I asked my advisor to show me what it would look like if I decided that I wanted to be pre-medicine. She responded enthusiastically and showed me exactly what I needed. Now, it was not in that moment that I knew I was going to switch to medicine a year later and go on a medical mission trip to south Africa which gave me a perspective how wonderful the feeling is to help someone out with their health and the reward you gain when they smile and say thank you. But, that’s how it turned out. I took a few leaps of faith along the way and was able to graduate from the Ohio State University with a BA degree in Arts and Sciences in a specialized study program for pre-medicine.

I then further advanced my education at Ohio University, where I received excellent education in clinical care and medicine surrounded by top notch peers in my class. I truly had a wonderful experience doing rotations in and around Columbus, OH learning from some of the best and brightest faculty preceptors in multiple disciplines. I can cite many stories of people I met along the way and experiences that shaped my skills and medical knowledge. My next stop was a transitional year residency at Unity Health in Searcy, Arkansas. As a guy who had a thing for the South, I am glad I finally was able to experience the slower pace and friendliness of the people. I was able to refine my abilities and this is where application of knowledge was finally utilized. I grew in understanding on how to be efficient and communicate with patients to instill confidence and explain to them their treatments. I also realized the responsibility I had to perform well and thrived on this opportunity with gratefulness.
